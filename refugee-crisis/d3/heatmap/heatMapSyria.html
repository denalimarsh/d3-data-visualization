This heat map shows refugees from the Syrian Arab Republic over the course of the ongoing civil war, visualizing data from January 2013 - April 2017. The visualization shows us that civillians turned refugees have borne the weight of this conflict, as major events in the war coincide with an increase in refugees. The refugee count steadily increases throughout 2014, as ISIS captures huges swathes of territory (the American led coalition began airstrikes in August 2014). Rising casualties among regime forces in July-August 2015 led to Russian intervention in September 2015. The dark brown months of September-December 2015 saw the regime push back rebel troops with the support of Russian airstrikes, heavy fighting continued well into 2016. In late 2016-2017, refugee flows have slowed as fighting wanes.
