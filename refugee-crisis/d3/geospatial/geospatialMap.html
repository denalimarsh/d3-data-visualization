This interactive geospatial map shows refugees accepted by country in 2013, giving a holistic world view of which countries are actively bearing the weight of the crisis. Hovering over countries shows how many refugees were accepted during the 2013 calander year, and clicking on any country allows you to zoom in and see how it compares to its neighbors. Here we see refugees from Iraq and Syria flowing into surrounding countries such as Iran, Jordan, Turkey, Lebanon, and Egypt. Of note are Middle Eastern countries in the same region with relatively few accepted refugees, such as Saudi Arabia, Qatar, United Arab Emirates, Oman, and Israel. Germany begins accepting refugees en masse, although Angela Merkel's "open door policy" would not be enacted until 2015.
